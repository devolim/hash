Getting Started

Download Hash for free from the App Store (iOS) or Google Play (Android). The app is free and will always remain free. No in-app purchases, no subscriptions.
No. Hash does not ask for any phone number, email address, or password. On first launch, you create a local PIN and an anonymous Hash ID is automatically generated. That's it.
Your Hash ID is a unique and anonymous identifier assigned to your device. It is not linked to any personal information. No one can trace back to you using your Hash ID.
Two methods are available:

1. QR Code (recommended) — One of you displays their QR code in the app, the other scans it. The connection is instant and anonymous.

2. Temporary code — Hash generates a 6-digit code valid for 5 minutes. Share it with your contact, and they enter it in the app to add you.
Hash operates on the principle of "one device = one account". This strengthens your security: your encryption keys only exist on a single device. You can however transfer your account to a new device through the settings.

Messages

Yes. As soon as your recipient receives a message, it is immediately deleted from our servers. If the recipient does not connect within 24 hours, the message is automatically and permanently destroyed. No copy is retained.
An ephemeral media is a photo or video that your recipient can only view once. Once viewed, it disappears permanently. It is impossible to view again or save it.
You can send photos, videos, documents, voice messages, and files of any type. All files are encrypted with AES-256-GCM before sending and deleted from the server after receipt.
Hash uses the Signal Protocol for end-to-end encryption of your messages. Each message is encrypted on your device before being sent. The server only sees an unreadable block of data. Only your recipient has the key to decrypt it. Furthermore, the Sealed Sender protocol ensures that even the server does not know who is sending what to whom.
Yes. Hash allows you to share your location in an encrypted manner. Your position is encrypted in the same way as your messages and deleted from the server after receipt.

Calls

Yes. All audio and video calls are end-to-end encrypted. The connection is made directly between devices (peer-to-peer). No recording is made. No call duration or call history is retained.
Call quality depends on your internet connection. For the best experience:

• Use a stable Wi-Fi connection
• Make sure your 4G/5G connection is sufficient
• Close other applications consuming bandwidth
• Verify that both participants have a good connection
Yes. Hash supports both audio and video calls. Both are end-to-end encrypted. You can switch between audio and video during a call.

Security

Vash Mode is a feature unique to Hash. You set up a second PIN code. If someone forces you to open the app, you enter this second code. The app opens normally but all your data is silently and irreversibly deleted — contacts, messages, conversations, files. The observer sees an empty app. No alert is displayed.

Warning: this action is irreversible. All data is permanently lost.
The panic button allows you to instantly and permanently delete all application data in a single action. Unlike Vash Mode, this deletion is visible — the user explicitly confirms the destruction of their data.
Shake-to-lock allows you to instantly lock the application by shaking your phone. Useful if someone approaches you while you are using Hash. You can adjust the detection sensitivity in the settings.
Sealed Sender is a protocol that prevents the server from knowing who is communicating with whom. Your messages travel completely anonymously: the sender, the recipient, and the content are invisible to our infrastructure. Even if someone intercepts the traffic, they cannot determine who is sending a message to whom.
By design, Hash stores no information on our servers that would allow resetting your PIN. If you have configured a recovery phrase, you can use it to regain access. Otherwise, the application data is irrecoverably lost and you will need to reinstall Hash to start fresh. This is a security choice, not a flaw.
No. End-to-end encryption means that only you and your recipient can read your messages. The server only sees encrypted data that it is technically unable to decrypt. Furthermore, the Sealed Sender protocol prevents the server from knowing who is communicating with whom. Even if we received a legal order, we would be unable to provide the content of your conversations.

Notifications

Go to Settings > Notifications and change the displayed name. You can set it to "Reminder", "Notes", or any name you like. Notifications will display this name instead of "Hash", making the app invisible in everyday life.
In Settings > Notifications, enable the option to hide content. Notifications will display a generic message instead of the actual content of your messages. No one will be able to read your conversations over your shoulder.
Check the following points:

• Permissions — Make sure Hash has permission to send notifications (Phone Settings > Hash > Notifications)
• Do Not Disturb mode — Check that it is not enabled
• Battery — Some phones disable notifications in power-saving mode. Exclude Hash from battery optimization
• Connection — Check your internet connection

Account

Go to Settings > Account > Transfer my account. The app generates a transfer code. Enter this code on your new device. The transfer moves your Hash ID and contacts to the new device. The old device is automatically disconnected.

Note: messages are not transferred, in keeping with Hash's ephemeral message principle.
Go to Settings > Account > Delete my account. Deletion is immediate and irreversible. All your data (Hash ID, contacts, encryption keys, messages) is permanently erased from your device and our servers.

You can also request deletion by email: hash-support@devolim.fr

More information on our account deletion page.
No. Deleting a Hash account is permanent and irreversible. No data is retained on our servers after deletion. This is a design choice to guarantee your privacy — if we retain nothing, no one can access it.
None. Hash collects strictly no personal data. No phone number, no email, no IP address, no metadata, no analytics, no cookies, no trackers. The entire codebase is open source and verifiable by anyone. See our privacy policy for more details.

Troubleshooting

Check your internet connection. Messages are sent as soon as the connection is restored. If the problem persists, try closing and reopening the app. If your contact has deleted their account or blocked you, the message cannot be delivered.
Try the following steps:

• Make sure you have the latest version of Hash
• Restart your phone
• Check that your operating system is up to date
• If the problem persists, contact us at hash-support@devolim.fr describing the issue
Send an email to hash-support@devolim.fr with a description of the issue, your device model, and your operating system version. Screenshots or a detailed description of the steps to reproduce the bug are very helpful.
