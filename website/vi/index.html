ZERO-KNOWLEDGE

EVEN WE KNOW NOTHING.

Hash uses the "Sealed Sender" protocol: the server relays your messages without ever knowing who sends them or who receives them. Zero metadata. Zero logs.

1

You send a message

Your message is encrypted on your device with the Signal Protocol. Only the recipient has the key to decrypt it.

2

The server sees nothing

The message arrives in an anonymous mailbox. The server doesn't know who sent it, nor what it contains.

3

The recipient receives it

The message is decrypted locally. A delivery receipt is sent automatically.

4

The message disappears

As soon as receipt is confirmed, the message is permanently deleted from the server. No copy is kept, anywhere.

EPHEMERAL MESSAGES

AFTER 24H, NOTHING EXISTS.
BY DESIGN.

Hash is not a traditional messaging app. Here, no conversation lasts. Messages are encrypted, sent, received — then permanently deleted.

1

ENCRYPTED SENDING

2
